Scalability with realism

Designed for global reach and infrastructure asymmetry

Browser-first delivery

The program should run in standard web environments so schools do not need advanced local installations to participate.

Multilingual support

English, Spanish, Portuguese, and Hebrew should be first-wave interfaces, with content localization built into the challenge workflow.

Local challenge selection

Schools choose from globally shared templates and then adapt examples, evidence sources, and outputs to their own context.

Important fix in the narrative

Do not present inequality in infrastructure as already solved. Present it as a known implementation challenge with a practical mitigation strategy: lightweight delivery, phased adoption, and prioritized support for lower-resource contexts.
